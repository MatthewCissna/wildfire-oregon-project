Oregon Wildfire ML

Interactive atlas · risk, fire counts & satellite detection · 2001–2024

An honest, end-to-end wildfire system for Oregon

A reproducible pipeline trained on — real cell-weeks of MODIS fire labels, GRIDMET weather, terrain, fuel and NDVI (2001–2024), plus NIFC ignition cause. Three deliverables — a per-cell risk surface, per-region fire-count forecasts, and a satellite burn-scar detector — all evaluated with leakage-free cross-validation.

The headline finding

Predicting weekly fire in a 36 km² cell from environment alone is genuinely hard — absolute scores are low for every method. The real win is spatial generalization: under leave-one-region-out cross-validation the gradient-boosted model beats a climatology baseline ~4×, while climatology collapses to no-skill (ROC-AUC 0.50). The model transfers to regions it never trained on; "where fires usually happen" does not.

Two tempting-but-inflated results were caught and rejected along the way — a fire-persistence shortcut (PR-AUC 0.65) and a trivially-separable synthetic detector (1.00). The reported numbers are lower and trustworthy.

⚠️ The honest interpretation

We deliberately exclude fire_lag1 (did this cell burn last week?). On real data fires persist week-to-week, so it dominates and inflates PR-AUC to ~0.65 — but that is fire continuation, not risk. The numbers above are the honest environmental skill. They are modest in absolute terms because weekly cell-level fire is rare and hard, but the GBM beats both baselines on both CV schemes, and ~4× climatology spatially — the meaningful, generalizable result.

Pipeline at a glance

  1. Ingest — Earth Engine (MODIS burned area + thermal, GRIDMET, SRTM, WorldCover, NDVI, GRIDMET drought) + NIFC ignition cause, on an H3 grid.
  2. Features — antecedent weather windows, dryness memory, interactions, NDVI anomaly, ignition-cause priors.
  3. Models — LightGBM risk surface, negative-binomial counts, EfficientNet burn-scar CNN.
  4. Evaluation — spatial-block / leave-region-out / forward-chaining CV; PR-AUC, Brier, recall@threshold; SHAP.
